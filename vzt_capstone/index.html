Safe Driver Training Program

This course will inform the City of Tacoma employees of safe driving habits, hazard perception, and responsibly driving a COT motor pool vehicle for work purposes.
Through interactive videos, assessments, and visual information, you will reconnect with safe driving best practices and habits.

During an interactive real world driving simulation, you will develop and strengthen hazard perception skills to enhance on the road awareness. Through information recall, feedback, and job aids, you will continue to review and practice driver safety after the modules are complete

This course will include a series of modules to introduce current statistics, inform of the latest safe driving techniques, list ways to increase hazard perception, and allow for practicing new knowledge through assessment and interactive learning, and allow for each driver to use a checklist to prepare and manage driving a city vehicle

Employees who complete the course successfully (above 80% assessment) will be certified by the Vision Zero Tacoma Driver’s Training Certification Program. This will allow them to submit a form to their supervisors prior to reserving and using City of Tacoma motor pool vehicles.

Please complete each of the modules in order and finish each module before continuing on to the next.

By the end of this entire program, you will:

    • Recall statistical information about crash data in Tacoma and the United States.
    • Describe the goals of Vision Zero Tacoma.
    • Summarize the traditional approach to automobile crash fatalities and serious injuries and the informed Vision Zero Tacoma approach.
    • Order steps introduced in a safe driving pre-drive check list from a list.
    • Practice filling out a Driver’s Check list job aid to use before driving a fleet car.
    • Compare current driving habits to hazard perception skills.
    • Apply hazard perception skills to real-world driving situations.
    • Determine developing hazards in a video driving simulation.

Module 1: Vision Zero Tacoma

This module includes an introduction to Vision Zero Tacoma and provide national and local statistics of vehicle and pedestrian fatalities and severe crashes. It will act as the foundation and introduction to the learning present in the subsequent modules. This will include a knowledge check based on the recalling statistical information provided.

BEGIN

Module 2: Driver Safety Best Practices

This module will review and reinforce driver safety best practices based on guidelines created by state and local driving rules and regulations. You can evaluate and reinforce best practices and examine your own driving experience and skills. You will focus on concepts of distracted driving, road awareness, and ways to make sure you are safe even before starting the vehicle. This module includes an online accessible or printable checklist.

BEGIN

Module 3: Hazard Perception

This module introduces the concept of hazard perception and show how training can enhance the skills and abilities even the most experienced drivers. This will be followed by creating good habits, skill building, and practice methods and periodic knowledge checks.

BEGIN

Module 4: Video Simulation

This final module is a video simulation of real-world based scenarios of potentially hazardous driving situations. You will use newly acquired knowledge and past driving experience to recognize Potential and Developing hazards to gain points to self-evaluate your learning based on a point system at the completion of the simulation.
