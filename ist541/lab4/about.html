About the Gator

Gator Lanphear

I will fill this with information about Gator Lanphear in the weeks to come Gator Lanphear, 50, lives on Vashon Island, WA with 4 kids (two step 18, 17, and twin Dragons - 9, 9), his partner Kimberly (not 50), 3 dogs, 2 cats, turtle, fish, chickens.

He is finishing his MS at CSU - Monterey Bay in Instructional Design (MIST).

Gator was born on Guam, grew up in Sacramento (SHS class of 1990), and is an Artist/Educator for the City of Tacoma.

He loves hockey (Go Sharks!), rain, and Korean dramas.

Gator strives to be anti-racist/anti-white supremacist.

He is known for saying, "I like to swing the hammer of discomfort - Safe for white people means comfort, safe for BIPOC means staying alive."

He received a BA in animation and video production from The Evergreen State College, worked in sports television for 10 years, and received a teaching credential through Fresno State. Gator was also a kindergarten teacher.

Gator enjoys single malt Scotch on the the rocks once in a while, pot stickers, baking pies for his family, and designing T-shirts.
